Lesson 1 - Who Are You?
🎧 Lesson Audio — Listen before class
Listen & Answer:
1) What is the woman's name?
2) What is the man's name?
3) And you? Say: 'Hello, I am...'
✍️ Before next class (10 min): Record a 20-second voice note: 'Hello, I am... I am from...' • Write your 4-line introduction in your notebook • Listen to the lesson audio again before next class
VOCABULARY
• I am / I'm
• You are / You're
• He is / He's
• She is / She's
• It is / It's
• We are / We're
• They are / They're
• Name · nombre
• Student · estudiante
• Teacher · maestro/a
• Man · hombre
• Woman · mujer
• Boy · niño
• Girl · niña
• Class · clase
CONTEXT
Phase 1 (TPR): Teacher points to self: 'I'. Points to camera: 'You'. Students mimic.

Phase 2 (Visual Drill): Teacher shows photos (Man, Woman, Group). Students shout 'He!', 'She!', 'They!'.

Phase 3 (Chat Drop): 'Type I AM and your name in the chat. Hit enter on 3, 2, 1!'
STRUCTURE
Formula: Subject Pronoun + To Be + Noun

Explanation:
We use 'To Be' to talk about identity.
Full Form: I am John. She is Mary.
Contraction: I'm John. She's Mary.

🚨 Common Error:
Spanish speakers often drop the subject.
WRONG: Is Mary.
RIGHT: She is Mary.
EXAMPLES
The Virtual Party (10 mins)

Context: A Zoom networking event.

Task:
One by one, students unmute.
Student A: 'Hello, I am [Name]. I am a student.'
Teacher: 'Class, who is he/she?'
Class: 'He/She is [Name]!'

Challenge: Introduce a family member passing in the background. 'He is my brother.'
EXERCISES
Tier 1: Formula Completion
1. ____ am a student.
2. She ____ a teacher.
3. They ____ boys.

Tier 2: The Correction Game
Teacher writes wrong sentences on screen. Students must fix them.
- 'He are David.' -> 'He IS David.'
- 'I is a woman.' -> 'I AM a woman.'

VOCABULARY

• City · ciudad
• From · de / desde
• Where · dónde
• What · qué
• World · mundo
• Flag · bandera
• Map · mapa
• Passport · pasaporte
• Home · casa / hogar
• USA
• UK
• China
• India
• Spain
CONTEXT
Phase 1 (Flag Flash): Teacher holds up a famous flag. First student to type the country name wins.

Phase 2 (Where is it?): Show a famous landmark (Eiffel Tower). 'Where is it?' -> 'It is in France.'

Phase 3 (The Passport): Everyone hold up a real or imaginary passport to the camera.
STRUCTURE
Formula: I am + from + [Country/City]

Question Form: Where + are + you + from?

Explanation:

I am from Mexico. / I am from Madrid.

🚨 Common Error:
WRONG: I am of Spain.
RIGHT: I am from Spain.

2) Is Leo a teacher?
3) And you? Say: 'I am a...' or 'I am an...'
✍️ Before next class (10 min): Write the jobs of 3 people you know: 'My father is a...' • Practice: a + consonant, an + vowel with 5 jobs • Listen to the lesson audio again
VOCABULARY
• A (consonant)
• An (vowel)
• Doctor · doctor/a
• Teacher · maestro/a
• Nurse · enfermero/a
• Engineer · ingeniero/a
• Driver · conductor/a
• Chef · chef / cocinero
• Artist · artista
• Police · policía
• Worker · trabajador/a
• Boss · jefe/a
• Office · oficina
• Hospital · hospital
• School · escuela
CONTEXT
Phase 1 (Charades): Teacher mimes a job (driving, cooking, writing). Students guess the word.

Phase 2 (A or An?): Teacher shouts a job. Students show 1 finger for 'A' or 2 fingers for 'AN' to the camera.

Phase 3 (Workplace Match): 'Where does a doctor work?' -> 'Hospital.'
STRUCTURE
Formula: Subject + To Be + A/An + Job

Explanation:
Use 'A' before consonants (A doctor).
Use 'An' before vowels (An artist, An engineer).

🚨 Common Error:
In Spanish, we don't use articles for jobs.
WRONG: I am teacher.
RIGHT: I am a teacher.

✍️ Before next class (10 min): Write where 5 objects are in your room: in / on / under • Hide one object and write 2 clues in English • Listen to the lesson audio again
VOCABULARY
• In · en / dentro
• On · sobre / en
• Under · debajo de
• Next to · al lado de
• Behind · detrás de
• In front of · enfrente de
• Between · entre
• Near · cerca de
• Where · dónde
• Here · aquí
• There · allá / ahí
• Left · izquierda

• Place · lugar

CONTEXT

STRUCTURE

Formula: There is (1) / There are (2+)

Explanation:

🚨 Common Error:
In Spanish, 'Hay' is used for both singular and plural. In English, we MUST divide them.
WRONG: There is two books.
RIGHT: There are two books.
