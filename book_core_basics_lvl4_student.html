Lesson 1 - The Mechanics (S+V) & The Pronunciation War
🎧 Lesson Audio — Listen before class
Listen & Answer:
1) What is wrong with the first sentence Marcos wrote?
2) What two things does every English sentence need?
3) And you? Say a complete sentence: subject plus verb.
✍️ Before next class (10 min): Write 5 complete sentences about your day (underline S and V) • Find one Spanish sentence with no subject; translate it WITH subject • Listen to the lesson audio again
VOCABULARY
• Subject · who or what does the action
• Verb · the action or state word
• Minimal Pair · two words differing in one sound (ship/sheep)
• Spelling · writing words with the correct letters
• Pronunciation · how a word sounds when spoken
• Sentence · a complete idea with subject + verb
• Hero · the main positive character
• Action
• Goal · what you want to achieve
• Duel · a fight between two people
• War · armed conflict between countries
• Order · the sequence things go in
• Winner · the person who wins
• Score · the points achieved
• Points
CONTEXT
The Spelling Duel (5-10 mins):
Teacher vs Student or Student vs Student. Teacher says a word (e.g., 'CAT', 'BED', 'RUN'). The first one to type it CORRECTLY in the chat gets a point.
Round 2: Speed coding. Teacher types 'runs dog the'. First to fix it ('The dog runs') wins 2 points.
STRUCTURE
The Invisible Base (S+V):
In English, order is EVERYTHING. The Hero (Subject) must always act (Verb) before the Goal (Object).
The Master Table:
SUBJECT (Hero)VERBO (Action)
The Playerwins
The Teamscores
The Bosscommands
The Robotexecutes

If you flip the order, the game breaks!
EXAMPLES
Robot Command Center (20 mins):
One student is the 'Commander', another is the 'Robot'.
The Commander can ONLY use S+V instructions. 'Robot takes box.' 'Robot moves left.'
If the Commander says 'Moves robot', the Robot must 'short circuit' and stop. How many tasks can the Robot complete in 2 minutes?
EXERCISES
Pronunciation War: A vs E (15 mins):
Divide the class into 'Team A' and 'Team E'.
• Team A must pronounce words with A (BAD, BAT, MAT).
• Team E must pronounce words with E (BED, BET, MET).
Points are awarded for clarity and speed. Use a timer! Use the chat to 'attack' with words for the other team to pronounce.

Lesson 2 - Punctuation Marks
🎧 Lesson Audio — Listen before class
Listen & Answer:

✍️ Before next class (10 min): Rewrite one of your old texts fixing punctuation • Find a comma splice in your writing and repair it • Listen to the lesson audio again
VOCABULARY
• Period · the . mark that ends a sentence
• Comma · the , mark — separates items, never joins two full sentences
• Question mark · the ? mark ending every question
• Exclamation mark · the ! mark for strong emotion
• Apostrophe · the ' mark for possession or contractions
• Quote · repeating someone's exact words
• Colon · the : mark — introduces a list or explanation
• Dash · the — mark, adds dramatic extra information
• Bracket · the ( ) marks for extra info
• Hyphen · the - mark joining words (well-known)
• Slash · the / mark meaning 'or'
• Dot
• Space · empty gap between words
• Mark · a symbol or sign; to grade
• Sign · to write your name to confirm
CONTEXT
Kung Fu Punctuation:
Punch (.) Chop (,) Uppercut (?). Teacher reads a text, students act out the punctuation marks physically.
Alternative: 'Chaos Readings'. Teacher reads a coherent text with ZERO pauses or intonation. Students realize why punctuation matters.
STRUCTURE
The Big 4 (+1):
. (Period/Full Stop): End of thought.
, (Comma): Pause, list, or linking two clauses.
? (Question): Asking.
! (Exclamation): Emotion (Use sparingly!).
' (Apostrophe): Possession 's or Contraction (don't).
EXAMPLES
Dictation Speed:
Teacher reads fast. Students type. Must be 100% accurate with caps and marks.
Scenario B: 'Text Message Translator'. Convert 'c u l8r' text speak into formal, punctuated English.
EXERCISES
The Unpunctuated Text:
Block of text on screen. Students use 'Stamp' tool to add dots and commas.
Extension: 'Eats Shoots and Leaves'. Show how commas change meaning. 'Let's eat, Grandpa' vs 'Let's eat Grandpa'.

Lesson 4 - Linking Words (And, But, So)
🎧 Lesson Audio — Listen before class
Listen & Answer:
1) Which connector shows a contrast: and, but, or so?
2) How does Tomas connect 'It rained' and 'we stayed home'?
3) And you? Make one sentence using 'so'.
✍️ Before next class (10 min): Write 4 sentences, one per connector, about your week • Combine 3 short sentences from a previous homework into 1 • Listen to the lesson audio again
VOCABULARY
• And
• But
• So
• Because
• Or
• Also · in addition
• However · but — formal contrast
• Therefore · so — formal result
• Example · a specific case that proves your point
• Result · what happens because of something
• Reason · why something happens or is true
• Choice · an option you can pick
• Addition · adding one more idea (and, also)
• Contrast · to show how two things are different
• Link
CONTEXT
Chain Story:
Student A: 'I went out...' B: 'BUT it rained...' C: 'SO I bought an umbrella.' D: 'BECAUSE I hate getting wet.'
Alternative: 'Conjunction Tennis'. Teacher: 'But!' Student: 'I tried, but I failed.'
STRUCTURE
Connection Logic:
And (+): Addition.
But (-): Contrast (Comma before).
However: Contrast (Start sentence. Comma after).
Example: 'It is cold, but I like it.' vs 'It is cold. However, I like it.'
EXAMPLES
Debate Opening:
'I agree with X, BUT we must consider Y. SO, we should do Z.'
Scenario B: 'Review Writing'. Review a movie. 'The acting was good, but the story was bad. So I give it 3 stars.'
EXERCISES
Sentence Combiner:
Join sentences. 'I like tea. I don't like coffee.' -> 'I like tea, but I don't like coffee.'
Extension: 'The Excuse Generator'. 'I can't come BECAUSE...' 'I want to come, BUT...'
