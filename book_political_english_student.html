Political & Global Affairs (A1-C2)

To the Teacher:

This course prepares students for international relations, diplomacy, and governance. The activities are designed for Online Simulation using tools like Screen Annotation, Breakout Rooms, and Chat.

Virtual Tools:

  • The Chat Box: Use for drafting treaties, press releases, and secret diplomatic cables.
  • Screen Share: Display maps for border disputes and photos for conflict analysis.
  • Mute Button: Use selectively to simulate 'Censorship' or 'Filibustering'.

Leaders & Roles

Context / Warm Up
Famous Face-Off (Screen Share):
Show cropped photos of leaders (eyes only). 'Who is this?' 'It is Macron.'
Theory / Concept
1. Core Vocabulary:
PresidentPrime MinisterKingQueenLeaderBossCountryFlagVotePeoplePowerNameTitleGovernmentLaw

2. Concept: To Be + Titles:
He is the President.
She is Queen Elizabeth (No 'The').
I am a voter.
Exercises / Drills
Role Reveal:
Assign roles in private chat (King, Spy, Soldier). Students ask 'Are you the King?'
Application / Project
The Gala Line-Up:
Virtual Reception. Students introduce themselves. 'Hello, I am [Name], the [Role] of [Country].'
