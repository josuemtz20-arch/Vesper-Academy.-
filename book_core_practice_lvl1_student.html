Welcome to Core Practice: Level 1!

This book helps you practise real, everyday English (A1–A2): your routine, free time, travel, work and more — 20 lessons, each about 60 minutes.

How to use this book:

  • Warm Up: Answer the questions out loud. Don’t worry about mistakes — just speak.
  • Vocab Drill: Learn the 12 key words, then make your own sentence in “Your turn”.
  • Listening / Reading: Odd lessons have audio — press play and listen twice. Even lessons have a short text to read.
  • Comprehension & Guided Practice: Do the exercises, then check yourself with the green Answer key.
  • Free Output: Speak or write freely — this is where your English grows. Studying alone? Look for the blue “Studying alone?” tips.

Tip: a little practice every day beats one long session a week. Good luck!

Lesson 1: Morning Routine

Warm Up
Warm up your English: answer these out loud, then write one full sentence for each.

1. First action: What is the very first thing you do when you wake up? (e.g., look at your phone, drink water)

2. The alarm: Do you wake up with an alarm or naturally? Are you a morning person?

3. Speed: How fast can you get ready in the morning when you are late?
Vocab Drill
Core Arsenal (12 words):
1. Wake up (Stop sleeping)
2. Get up (Leave the bed)
3. Alarm (A clock that makes a loud noise)
4. Shower (Wash your body)
5. Brush (Clean teeth or hair)
6. Breakfast (The first meal of the day)
7. Leave (Go out of the house)
8. Arrive (Reach a place)
9. Early (Before the usual time)
10. Late (After the usual time)
11. Tired (Needing sleep)
12. Routine (Things you do every day)

Your turn: Say (and write) one sentence using wake up and early. Example: “I wake up early on Mondays.”
Main Listening Input
Press play and listen twice. The words are not printed here on purpose — train your ears! Then answer the questions below.
Comprehension Check
Level 1 (Literal):
- What time is it in the story?
- Does John take a shower?

Level 2 (Inference):
- Why is John tired?
- Why does John decide to buy a coffee instead of eating at home?

Level 3 (Applied Opinion):
- Is it bad to skip breakfast in the morning?
Guided Practice
Exercise 1 — Verb + noun: Choose the right verb: brush · take · eat · catch
a) ____ a shower   b) ____ your teeth   c) ____ breakfast   d) ____ the bus

Exercise 2 — Telling the time: Write each time in words.
a) 7:00   b) 7:30   c) 7:45   d) 8:15

Exercise 3 — Dictation: Play the audio again and write the first sentence you hear. Then check your spelling.
Answer key
1. a) take  b) brush  c) eat  d) catch. 2. a) seven o’clock  b) half past seven  c) quarter to eight  d) quarter past eight. 3. Compare your sentence with the audio and fix any missing words.
Free Output Simulation
The Late Worker

Situation: You arrive very late to work and the boss is not happy.

Role A — The Boss: You are annoyed. Ask why the worker is late and what time they woke up.
Role B — The Worker: Explain your terrible morning. Say your alarm didn’t ring and you had no time for breakfast. Apologise.

Goal: Keep the conversation going for about 3 minutes.
Studying alone? Play both roles yourself, or record a 60–90 second voice note as the worker apologising. Start with: “I’m so sorry I’m late. My alarm didn’t ring, so I woke up at…”

Lesson 2: Weekend Activities

Warm Up
Warm up your English: answer these out loud, then write one full sentence for each.

1. The Best Day: Which day is better: Saturday or Sunday? Why?

2. Indoor vs Outdoor: On the weekend, do you prefer to stay inside or go outside?

3. The Chore: What is one boring thing you MUST do every weekend? (e.g., wash clothes).
Vocab Drill
Core Arsenal (12 words):
1. Weekend (Saturday and Sunday)
2. Relax (Rest and do nothing hard)
3. Busy (Having a lot to do)
4. Clean (Remove dirt)
5. Shop (Buy things)
6. Visit (Go to see a person or place)
7. Park (A large green place in a city)
8. Cinema (A place to watch movies)
9. Enjoy (To like doing something)
10. Boring (Not interesting)
11. Fun (Enjoyable and happy)
12. Together (With another person)

Your turn: Say (and write) one sentence using relax and weekend. Example: “I relax with my family every weekend.”
Main Reading Input
PART 1: Saturday Chores
For Mary, the weekend is a time to relax, but Saturday morning is always busy. First, she gets up at eight o'clock and cleans her small apartment. She washes the dishes and sweeps the floor. Then, she goes to the supermarket to shop for food. This is very boring, but she must do it.

PART 2: Sunday Fun
Sunday is a fun day. Mary visits her parents in the afternoon. They live near a big park. They walk in the park together and enjoy the fresh air. In the evening, Mary sometimes goes to the cinema with her friends. She loves to watch funny movies before she goes back to work on Monday.
Comprehension Check
Level 1 (Literal):
- What does Mary do on Saturday morning?
- Who does Mary visit on Sunday?

Level 2 (Inference):
- Why does Mary think shopping for food is 'boring'?
- Why does Mary like funny movies on Sunday evening?

Level 3 (Applied Opinion):
- Do you think it is better to do all your chores on Saturday or spread them out during the week?
Guided Practice
Exercise 1 — Opposites: Write the opposite of each adjective: relaxed · fun · small
a) busy – ____   b) boring – ____   c) big – ____

Exercise 2 — Present simple: Rewrite each sentence. Change I to She, change my to her, and add -s or -es to the verb.
a) I clean my room.   b) I shop for food.   c) I watch funny movies.   d) I go to the cinema.

Exercise 3 — Spelling: Write the missing letters. Then find the three words in the reading to check.
a) su_erma_ket   b) apa_tme_t   c) c_nem_
Answer key
1. a) relaxed  b) fun  c) small. 2. a) She cleans her room.  b) She shops for food.  c) She watches funny movies.  d) She goes to the cinema. 3. a) supermarket  b) apartment  c) cinema.
Free Output Writing
My Perfect Weekend

Prompt: Write about your ideal weekend.

Task: Write 2 short paragraphs.
- Paragraph 1: Write 3 sentences about what you do on Saturday. Use the words shop or clean.
- Paragraph 2: Write 3 sentences about Sunday. Who do you visit? How do you relax?
Model answer
On Saturday, I clean my apartment in the morning. Then I shop for food at the supermarket. In the afternoon, I play football with my friends.

On Sunday, I visit my grandmother. We eat lunch together and talk a lot. In the evening, I relax on the sofa and watch a funny movie.

I liked the movie a lot because the bear is sweet and the jokes are good. You should watch it with your family. It is not boring!

Mike has a very unhealthy diet. Every morning, he eats three donuts with a lot of sugar and drinks a large soda. For lunch, he always eats salty fast food like french fries and fried meat. He never drinks pure water. Because of his diet, Mike feels very tired all the time and his stomach hurts.

PART 2: The Change

Level 3 (Applied Opinion):

Guided Practice
Exercise 1 — Healthy or unhealthy: Sort these foods into two lists: Healthy or Unhealthy.
apple · donut · water · soda · carrot · fried chicken

Exercise 2 — Because / so: Join each pair of sentences with so.
a) Mike eats bad food. / He feels tired.
b) Mike drinks soda. / His stomach hurts.
c) Mike eats a vegetable every day. / He feels healthy.

Exercise 3 — Spelling: Unscramble the letters to make three words from the reading.
a) r-a-g-u-s   b) y-h-t-l-a-e-h   c) b-e-l-e-g-a-t-e-v
Answer key
1. Healthy: apple, water, carrot. Unhealthy: donut, soda, fried chicken. 2. a) Mike eats bad food, so he feels tired.  b) Mike drinks soda, so his stomach hurts.  c) Mike eats a vegetable every day, so he feels healthy. 3. a) sugar  b) healthy  c) vegetable.
Free Output Writing
The Diet Plan

Prompt: Write a healthy menu for one day.

Task: Write 2 short paragraphs.
- Paragraph 1: Describe what you will cook for breakfast and lunch. Include fruit and water.
- Paragraph 2: Describe a fresh dinner. What meat and vegetable will you eat? Explain why this diet is healthy.
Model answer
For breakfast, I will cook eggs and eat a piece of fruit, like an apple. I will drink a glass of water. For lunch, I will make a chicken salad and drink more water.

For dinner, I will cook fresh fish with a green vegetable, like broccoli. The meat is not fried. This diet is healthy because it has no sugar and no salty fast food.

b) The worker ____ (give) him a plastic key.

Exercise 2 — Two details: Make each sentence longer with two details. Example: The room was nice becomes The room was clean and the bed was large.
a) The breakfast was good.   b) The hotel was OK.   c) The view was nice.

Exercise 3 — Spelling: Write the missing letter in each hotel word. Then check your answers in the reading.
a) rece_tion   b) rese_vation   c) resta_rant

1. a) went  b) gave  c) took. 2. Model answers: a) The breakfast was free and the coffee was hot.  b) The hotel was small and the people were friendly.  c) The view was beautiful and I could see the city lights. 3. a) reception  b) reservation  c) restaurant.
Free Output Writing
The Hotel Review

Prompt: You stayed in a terrible hotel. Write a bad review online.

Task: Write 2 short paragraphs.
– Paragraph 1: Say how many nights you stayed. Complain about the room (was it dirty?).
– Paragraph 2: Complain about the bathroom and the reception desk. Tell people not to book this hotel.
Model answer
I stayed at this hotel for two nights, and it was terrible. The room was small and dirty, and the bed was very old.

The bathroom was worse. There was no hot water, and the shower didn’t work. The man at the reception desk was not friendly and did not help me. Please do not book this hotel!
