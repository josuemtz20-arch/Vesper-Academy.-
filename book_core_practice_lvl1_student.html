Course Overview:

Level 1 focuses on Survival & Social Confidence (A1-A2). It introduces the highly active Paired-Skills Methodology and the Deep Expansion format.

Classroom Flow:

  • Odd Lessons (Auditory): Speaking and Listening focus. Heavy emphasis on roleplay and dictation.
  • Even Lessons (Textual): Reading and Writing focus. Heavy emphasis on textual comprehension and guided writing tasks.

Lesson 1: Morning Routine

Warm Up
Phase 1: First Action: What is the very first thing you do when you wake up? (e.g., look at phone, drink water).

Phase 2: The Alarm: Do you wake up with an alarm or naturally? Are you a morning person?

Phase 3: Speed: How fast can you get ready in the morning if you are late?
Vocab Drill
Core Arsenal (12 words):
1. Wake up (Stop sleeping)
2. Get up (Leave the bed)
3. Alarm (A clock that makes a loud noise)
4. Shower (Wash your body)
5. Brush (Clean teeth or hair)
6. Breakfast (The first meal of the day)
7. Leave (Go out of the house)
8. Arrive (Reach a place)
9. Early (Before the usual time)
10. Late (After the usual time)
11. Tired (Needing sleep)
12. Routine (Things you do every day)

Drill Task: Say a sentence using 'Wake up' and 'Early'.
Main Listening Input
(The script text is hidden in the student manual. Listen to the audio closely to complete the exercise.)
Comprehension Check
Level 1 (Literal):
- What time is it in the story?
- Does John take a shower?

Level 2 (Inference):
- Why is John tired?
- Why does John decide to buy a coffee instead of eating at home?

Level 3 (Applied Opinion):
- Is it bad to skip breakfast in the morning?
Guided Practice
Exercise 1: Verb Matching: Match the verb to the noun: Brush (Teeth), Take (Shower), Eat (Breakfast).

Exercise 2: Telling Time: Practice saying 'Seven o'clock', 'Half past seven', 'Quarter to eight'.

Exercise 3: Dictation: Listen to Sarah's line: 'You must take a shower...' and write it down.
Free Output Simulation
The Late Worker (15 mins)

Context: You are very late for your job.

Role A (The Boss): You are angry. Ask the worker why they are late. Ask what time they woke up.
Role B (The Worker): You must explain your terrible morning routine. Say your alarm didn't ring and you didn't have breakfast.

Task: Have a 3-minute conversation where the worker apologizes and explains their morning.

Lesson 2: Weekend Activities

Warm Up
Phase 1: The Best Day: Which day is better: Saturday or Sunday? Why?

Phase 2: Indoor vs Outdoor: On the weekend, do you prefer to stay inside or go outside?

Phase 3: The Chore: What is one boring thing you MUST do every weekend? (e.g., wash clothes).
Vocab Drill
Core Arsenal (12 words):
1. Weekend (Saturday and Sunday)
2. Relax (Rest and do nothing hard)
3. Busy (Having a lot to do)
4. Clean (Remove dirt)
5. Shop (Buy things)
6. Visit (Go to see a person or place)
7. Park (A large green place in a city)
8. Cinema (A place to watch movies)
9. Enjoy (To like doing something)
10. Boring (Not interesting)
11. Fun (Enjoyable and happy)
12. Together (With another person)

Drill Task: Write a sentence using 'Relax' and 'Weekend'.
Main Reading Input
PART 1: Saturday Chores
For Mary, the weekend is a time to relax, but Saturday morning is always busy. First, she gets up at eight o'clock and cleans her small apartment. She washes the dishes and sweeps the floor. Then, she goes to the supermarket to shop for food. This is very boring, but she must do it.

PART 2: Sunday Fun
Sunday is a fun day. Mary visits her parents in the afternoon. They live near a big park. They walk in the park together and enjoy the fresh air. In the evening, Mary sometimes goes to the cinema with her friends. She loves to watch funny movies before she goes back to work on Monday.
Comprehension Check
Level 1 (Literal):
- What does Mary do on Saturday morning?
- Who does Mary visit on Sunday?

Level 2 (Inference):
- Why does Mary think Saturday is 'boring'?
- Why does Mary like funny movies on Sunday evening?

Level 3 (Applied Opinion):
- Do you think it is better to do all your chores on Saturday or spread them out during the week?
Guided Practice
Exercise 1: Adjective Opposites: Busy vs Relaxed. Boring vs Fun. Big vs Small.

Exercise 2: Present Simple: Change 'I clean my room' to 'She cleans her room'. Practice adding the 's'.

Exercise 3: Spelling: Practice spelling 'Supermarket', 'Apartment', and 'Cinema'.
Free Output Writing
My Perfect Weekend (15 mins)

Prompt: Write about your ideal weekend.

Task: Write 2 short paragraphs.
- Paragraph 1: Write 3 sentences about what you do on Saturday. Use the words 'Shop' or 'Clean'.
- Paragraph 2: Write 3 sentences about Sunday. Who do you 'Visit'? How do you 'Relax'?

- Paragraph 2: Did you like it? Tell your friend if they should 'watch' it or if it is 'boring'.

Drill Task: Name a 'Sweet' 'Fruit'.
Main Reading Input
PART 1: A Bad Diet
Mike has a very unhealthy diet. Every morning, he eats three donuts with a lot of sugar and drinks a large soda. For lunch, he always eats salty fast food like french fries and fried meat. He never drinks pure water. Because of his diet, Mike feels very tired all the time and his stomach hurts.

PART 2: The Change
Mike visits a doctor. The doctor tells him to change his diet immediately. He must start cooking fresh food at home. He needs to eat a green vegetable with every dinner and eat a piece of fruit for dessert. If he drinks water instead of soda, he will feel much better and become healthy.

Level 2 (Inference):
- Why does Mike feel tired all the time?

Level 3 (Applied Opinion):
- Why is it so hard for people to stop eating unhealthy food?
Guided Practice
Exercise 1: Food Categories: Sort these into Healthy or Unhealthy: Apple, Donut, Water, Soda, Carrot, Fried Chicken.

Exercise 2: Because / So: Join the sentences: 'Mike eats bad food. Mike feels tired.' -> 'Mike eats bad food, so he feels tired.'

Exercise 3: Spelling: Practice spelling 'Vegetable', 'Healthy', and 'Sugar'.
Free Output Writing
The Diet Plan (15 mins)

Prompt: Write a healthy menu for one day.

Task: Write 2 short paragraphs.
- Paragraph 1: Describe what you will 'cook' for breakfast and lunch. Include 'fruit' and 'water'.
- Paragraph 2: Describe a 'fresh' dinner. What 'meat' and 'vegetable' will you eat? Explain why this diet is 'healthy'.

Exercise 1: Past Tense Irregulars: Go -> Went, Give -> Gave, Take -> Took. Practice these verbs.

- Paragraph 2: Complain about the 'bathroom' and the 'reception' desk. Tell people not to 'book' this hotel.
